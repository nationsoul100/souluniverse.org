Article 2(4) – Prohibition of the Use of Force:

This article prohibits states from using force against the territorial integrity or political independence of any other state, except in self-defense or with the authorization of the UN Security Council. This principle upholds the territorial sovereignty of states.

Article 2(7) – Non-Intervention:

The Charter explicitly limits the authority of the United Nations to intervene in matters that fall strictly within a state’s domestic jurisdiction. This clause respects the sovereignty of states over internal matters such as governance, legal systems, and economic policies, unless such matters threaten international peace. However, this principle is not absolute, as the UN can authorize interventions in extreme cases, such as genocide or crimes against humanity, under Chapter VII of the Charter
